Early Access Review

Jumping into a match with a ton of absolute legends screaming out lines from the 1997 Paul Verhoeven's Starship Troopers has been a dream come true. The first impression really puts you in the Battle of Klendathu, Velaka in this case, with a soundtrack that pays homage to the film. The memorable part of playing will always be the endgame where everyone needs to extract safely and respawning is disabled. Troopers scramble from their overrun makeshift base to the landing zone and do their best to revive or providing covering fire for their fellow comrades. A two minute timer ticks away while an onslaught of Arachnids make their way to the dropship and you see a few Heavy Troopers hunker down with their mobile bunkers and unload their MG's into a wall of bugs. Medics desperately running into the line of fire while praying their drone can revive a downed teammate and Assault Troopers trying their hardest to lay covering fire from above and below. It was chaos on another level and I haven't had this much fun since the modded Left 4 Dead servers that allowed 8v8 with buy options.

The game is definitely in Alpha stage, with only 2 playable game modes and 3 Classes to level up. Their equipment and weapons are limited but Medic seems to be the most versatile with mid to long range weapons(even a grenade launcher that clears up the field very well), extremely useful perks, and a great selection of grenade types to even the playing field.

The gunplay is ok, I find myself using the standard issue Morita MK.I rifle way more than anything else for that Starship Trooper Combat Scene feel. It has reliable damage and a decent 60 round clip, but the damage effect on bugs is minimal. You can see some bullet holes on the pinchers of the Arachnids but you don't feel the weight of how much damage you do to them. You also don't see a pile up of bugs while you shoot from a wall to really see the carnage your team is creating when defending.

There are plenty of bugs to kill and it does not get dull after 8 hours of play. You'll see plenty of Drones and Warrior Bugs, sprinkled with alittle bit of Gunner Bugs who are extremely dangerous if left alone. The real fight happens when a couple Plasma Bugs and Tiger Tank Arachnids appear that can soak up a ton of damage and dish it out to destroy your fortifications. I am hoping the developers add those flying bugs to really make things tense as it is!

I will update this review with Cons in the future because right now I just havent experienced any real issues that have deterred me from playing or requesting a refund.
