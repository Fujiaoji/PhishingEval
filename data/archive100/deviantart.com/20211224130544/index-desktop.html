Season's Greeting by Lonewolf-Sparrowhawk, literature

Season's Greeting

Starting at the end of November Something begins to happen. You first see it in the weather: Ever since summer ended It didn’t seem able to make up its mind, But now it’s grown quieter, Settling down into sleepy cold, As if tucking itself away for winter. By the start of December, everyone can tell There’s something in the air that changes how things feel— The twinkling stars look a little closer; Eyes are brighter, voices clearer, Music seems to fill the soul With just a bit more wonder, All the animals seem slightly more knowing, And all the things of Make-Believe Seem a little bit more real. What makes this happen? No one quite knows. It could be because of the approach of Christmas. It could be because of the Winter Solstice. It could be because of the coming New Year. It could be because of the very last full moon before December ends. Whatever the reason, It is this time of year When Magic just might really happen…
