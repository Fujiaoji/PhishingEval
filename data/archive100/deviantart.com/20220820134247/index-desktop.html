Ragdolls by RainInTheWillows, literature

Literature

Ragdolls

We are each of us rag dolls; our bodies made from pieces of experience, stitched together with love and pain, clothed in scraps from varying times and emotions. A touch of sadness forms the green cloth that covers your thigh. A bit of laughter tints the purplish lavender that lays across your shoulder. The throb of love creates the royal purple over your heart, a mixture of happiness and affection tinting the mix. The bluest of blues trickle down your torso, radiating happiness with every stripe. A bit of pink for the days that illness laid you low. A touch of orange for days when your mind is so very full. A bit of red for the days that just never seem to turn out right. A hint of yellow to represent your sorrows and grief. We are but patchwork people, wearing our lives on our skins. We dress in our emotions to let the world know how we've been. If a seam should pop, I'll always be near with thread and needle to patch you up again. The stitches are the bonds we allow
RainInTheWillows
