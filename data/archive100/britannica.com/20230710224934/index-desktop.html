Heatwaves Swell Higher

If it seems hotter than usual, it’s not just you. Monday was the warmest day ever recorded, at 17.01 °C (62.9 degrees °F). That record stood for 24 hours when it was broken on Tuesday—and again on Wednesday—with temperatures of 17.18 °C (62.6 degrees °F). We know it’s getting warmer, thanks to tools including the Keeling Curve, which tracks global carbon dioxide concentrations. What we don’t know is the extent of its impact on the Earth, though we’re feeling the effects this summer.

Ice Cream History

Sure it’s been hot lately, but that’s just one more reason to eat ice cream. In honor of July being National Ice Cream Month, here’s the scoop on summer’s favorite treat.
Ancient delights
Ice cream evolved from flavored ices popular with the Roman nobility in the 4th century BCE. The emperor Nero imported snow from the mountains and topped it with fruit juices and honey.
European flavors
Modern iced desserts were introduced into Europe by Marco Polo, who brought back descriptions of fruit ices from China. Buontalenti, a cook taken to France by Catherine de’ Medici, first prepared it for the French court.
Refined in Paris
In 1686 Francesco Procopio opened a café in Paris and began to sell ices and sherbets, which became so popular that other shops soon opened. Tortoni, owner of a café in late-18th-century Paris, is credited with developing cream ices.
