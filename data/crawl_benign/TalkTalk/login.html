      BROADBAND SPEED CODE OF PRACTICE

      In TalkTalk we have voluntarily signed the residential version of Ofcom’s Code of Practice. By this we are sharing speed information, for both upload and download, factors affecting speed,  upfront or after sale with our customers. For speeds in specific, they are based on actual performance metrics of customers on the same product, during peak hours and according to the Ofcom methodology, so that our customers can take an informed decision on selecting our products. These speeds are from TalkTalk to your router/hub and are not inclusive of Wi-Fi speeds between your hub and your wireless device.  More specifically we provide, in different touchpoints, the following:


      1. Speeds: Normally available (download and upload), minimum (download and upload),  maximum (download and upload), advertised/headline (download and upload).

      2. Traffic: Explanation around actual speeds, impacting factors, speeds required for common uses and any policy that could impact speed.


      The code gives the right to our customers to exit their contract without penalty if the speed falls below the Guaranteed Minimum Download Speed we promised and more specifically if the customer’s actual download speed falls below the minimum guaranteed download speed given at point of sale on a daily basis for at least three successive days, whether continuously or intermittently, after reporting of a speed problem, and we can’t fix the problem within 30 days. If you're a new customer on the Fibre 35/65 or Ultrafast 150/250, your line will go through a stabilisation period of 10 days, when your speeds may fluctuate. Read more details about how the methodology, our commitments and customer rights coming from our voluntary signing of the code.
